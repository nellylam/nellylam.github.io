In creating and redesigning the Code Habits website, I had to identify what are the problems I am trying to solve. What do I think is important for my users?

First, I want to make my blogs easy to find from the homepage. Second, the blogs must be easy to read. Less urgent, but also importantly, I want to let people know more about me. How does my website tell other's who I am? I figured, this is going to happen implicitly through my blogs.

When creating the wireframes, I sketched a few pages of thumbnails. I realize that the home page and the blog pages will be different.

On the homepage, I wanted large, easy to find, and graphically simple links to the Blog, Projects, and About pages. In the actual blog article page, I want the user to easily find their way to other articles. So I kept the nav bar at the top, and eventually I want to have drop down menus showing links to all the blog posts.

The last step would be to make my blog reponsive to different browser sizes and easily read on a mobile device.
